Social Trust and Learned Helplessness as Social Innovation Design Elements: A Case Study on the Spy Camera Detector Application “Relief’s Map”

This research examines how the spy camera detector application, Relief's Map, offers vague means to understand women's safety from spy cameras in Korea, and even has the potential to abuse women’s fear and learned helplessness on spy cameras. To design for empowering marginalized people, I urge the importance of acknowledging the complexity of oppressed group's social psychology that centers around fear, learned helplessness, and indignation. Used Ethnographic Research methodology, processed prototyping and user testing, and met the corporation for detailed information and insights.

College Students’ Social Psychologies of the 2018 MeToo Movement in South Korea

Since Feminism has risen as a national issue within South Korea for the first time since 2016, the MeToo movement in South Korea had repercussions all over the country. This case study is concerned with the social and psychological context of the MeToo Movement in 2018’s South Korea. In specific, it deals with the relationship between the individual’s perception of the method of disclosure of the MeToo movement and the individual’s social psychologies. In other words, this paper analyzed the dynamic relationship between minority groups’ social psychologies and the response to the MeToo movement.

Cross-Cultural Studies on Authenticity of HipHop Idol, BTS

This research examines the authenticity of BTS regarding their group identity— hip hop idol. Although their authenticity is distinguished from the hip hop culture that black American rappers have exclusively formed and developed, BTS’s own “street credibility,” or the social embeddedness, has been proven by their making life ethos melt in song lyrics and social/political opinions and messages. Overcoming the identity crisis by accepting their identity as the Korean hip hop idol, BTS has ripened into a group that conveys hip hop authenticity. To specify, BTS started pursuing hip hop within the context of the youth’s sufferings in Korean society's survival system. However, they have ultimately expanded the message by embracing the youths suffering from neoliberalism or social minority groups and wholeheartedly solacing people who go through personal hardships. BTS’s success is rooted in their sincere communication mechanism rooted heavily in black American hip hop artists’ ethos.
